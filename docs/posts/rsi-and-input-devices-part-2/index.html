This is part 2 of RSI and Input Devices.

Logitech MX Vertical Ergonomic Mouse#

At first I was rejected the idea of using the Logitech MX Vertical Ergonomic Mouse I had tried the one my roommate had and found the scroll-wheel to be subpar, though after continued pain from using my MX Ergo Plus I decided to buy one off amazon. Even though I didn’t care for the scroll-wheel on the mouse, I noticed the pain in my hand and wrist decreased, and after a few days pretty much went away. This wasn’t the result I was expecting, but it’s one I’ll take. It’s also nice to have a USB-C port on an input device rather than micro-USB. While I haven’t been using it for a long, and can’t say for sure it will solve my RSI issues, I’m tentatively accepting that it’s going to be my daily driver mouse.
